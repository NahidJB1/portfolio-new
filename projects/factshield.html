~/problem

Panic Travels Faster Than Proof

Fake news moves at the speed of panic — long before anyone gets the chance to check it. By the time a claim is confirmed false, it's usually already been shared thousands of times over.

Days, Not Seconds

Established fact-checkers like Snopes rely on human review that takes days — an eternity when a claim is already spreading in real time.

Manual Search Fatigue

Verifying a headline yourself means digging through multiple sources mid-scroll — friction most people won't push through before hitting share.

No Tamper-Proof Trail

Even a debunked claim can quietly resurface later — there's no permanent, public record proving what was actually found.

~/objective

Truth, Verified in Under 15 Seconds

The goal was to design and build a platform that returns a verdict on any claim in under 15 seconds — and once that verdict is delivered, it's permanently recorded on the blockchain, so it can never quietly be changed after the fact. ~/features

How FactShield Verifies a Claim

Every submission — pasted text, an article link, or a video URL — moves through the same AI-driven pipeline before a verdict is ever shown.

Claim-by-Claim Breakdown

Rather than a single true/false stamp, the AI splits an article into individual claims, cross-references each against trusted global sources, and returns a three-tier verdict — Real, Fake, or Suspicious — along with the evidence behind it.

Immutable Blockchain Records

Every verdict is written to the Polygon network, generating a public, tamper-proof transaction hash. Once it's recorded, no one — including the team — can quietly edit or delete it.

Live Misinformation Heatmap

Submissions feed a color-coded global heatmap tracking where specific topics — health, politics, war — are actively spreading, turning a personal verification tool into a public early-warning system.

~/architecture

Under the Hood

FactShield runs a three-step pipeline: submit content, let AI analyze and score it, then commit the verdict permanently to the blockchain. 

┌───────────────┐        ┌───────────────┐        ┌───────────────┐
│    SUBMIT     │  --->  │  AI ANALYSIS  │  --->  │  BLOCKCHAIN   │
│  Text or URL  │        │Extract & Score│        │Polygon Network│
│  Claim Input  │  <---  │3-Tier Verdict │  <---  │ tx hash proof │
└───────────────┘        └───────────────┘        └───────────────┘

The AI layer extracts and cross-checks every claim against trusted global sources before scoring it; the result is then committed to the Polygon network as a public, immutable transaction — visible to anyone through the platform's own blockchain explorer.
